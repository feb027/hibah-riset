## Bibliographic metadata

## Query extraction fields

- Problem: counting high-density objects quickly and accurately remains difficult; many advances are scattered and the field still faces real-world scenario, high-density, and real-time processing challenges.
- Contribution: proposes a three-tier standardized dataset taxonomy; introduces average pixel occupied by each object (APO) as a dataset clarity index; classifies 36 datasets; reviews classic and recent deep-learning methods; evaluates more than 110 algorithms.
- Method/solution: survey categorizes crowd-counting methods from a data-driven view, including multi-scale, single-column, multi-column, multi-task, attention, and weak-supervised networks; also organizes datasets by scale and clarity.
- Main results: gives a broad taxonomy of datasets, metrics, and algorithms; highlights occlusion, scale variation, illumination, and real-time crowd processing as persistent challenges; identifies integration of multiple methods as an important direction.
- Limitations: survey-level source; does not itself propose an implementable detector-tracker-counting pipeline; density-map crowd counting differs from ID/zone-based counting.
- Relevance: useful background for crowd-counting taxonomy, datasets, MAE/RMSE-style evaluation, and the distinction between density estimation and tracking-based people counting.
