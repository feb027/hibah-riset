## Bibliographic metadata

- Title: High-accuracy occupancy counting at crowded entrances for smart buildings
- Authors: Kailai Sun; Xinwei Wang; Tian Xing; Shaobo Liu; Qianchuan Zhao

## Query extraction fields

- Problem: occupancy counting at crowded entrances is difficult because occlusion, simultaneous arrivals/departures, and cumulative errors reduce counting accuracy; inaccurate counts can mislead occupant-centric building control.
- Contribution: proposes a deep-learning occupancy tracking method for crowded entrances; annotates an occupancy tracking dataset; fuses multi-camera information; designs occupant-centric control for energy saving.
- Method/solution: focuses on head tracking to reduce occlusion; trains MOT models; applies trajectory/counting logic; fuses two camera views; uses occupancy arrival/departure/increment information for control.
- Main results: reports approximately 98.1% occupancy counting accuracy using two real-scene surveillance cameras; reports 80.4% and 69.9% MOTA for two cameras; surpasses existing methods by 7.7%; estimates 17–25% room energy-saving potential.
- Limitations: dataset comes from a limited range of scenes and needs more diverse validation.
- Relevance: directly supports tracking-based entrance/zone people counting, head tracking under occlusion, multi-camera fusion, and practical occupancy counting evaluation.
